# User session report — v0.6.2

**topic:** why database indexes make reads fast and writes slow
**mode:** standard · **real minutes:** ~12 (of which **~7 were spent waiting**)
**nodes encoded:** 1 · **reviews cleared:** 1 · **loop closed:** yes

Run per `RELEASE_PROTOCOL.md` §5.6, in a throwaway `ENGRAM_HOME`, driving the real agents with
the exact payloads the real skills hand them.

---

## WHAT WORKED

- **The curriculum architect is the best thing in this product.** It returned an 18-node DAG that
  is a genuine chain of necessity — `page-unit-of-io †` → `random-vs-sequential` →
  `sorted-order-enables-logarithmic-search` → `index-is-a-redundant-sorted-copy †` →
  `fanout-makes-trees-shallow †` → … → `write-amplification-ledger` → `the-rum-tradeoff`. It
  flagged three threshold concepts, marked `the-constants` as `arbitrary` (memorize, don't
  derive), and wrote a `transfer_probe` that lands the same idea on CPU cache lines. Nothing in
  the plugin ecosystem does this.

- **The blind assessor is the second best thing.** Given a deliberately half-right, tired answer —
  *"it pulls the whole page not just the row, like 8kb. so counting rows is wrong, you should
  count pages"* — it graded **`partial`** and named **exactly** the missing rubric criterion:

  > *"You have the mechanism (the whole ~8KB page moves, not the row) and the accounting rule
  > (count pages), but never stated the step that joins them — that fetching this one 64-byte row
  > costs exactly the same I/O as fetching every row sharing its page — and that equivalence is
  > the whole reason pages are the unit worth counting."*

  That is a better piece of teaching than the lesson. It did not flatter, it did not round up,
  and it told me the one thing I was missing.

- **The numbers are honest and mutually consistent.** Ran every command against one state:
  `adherence` 1/1, `retention` early-bucket n=1 (correctly **not** counted as retention — a day-1
  retrieval is still encoding), `unmeasured` 0, `coverage` complete, `decay`, `stats`, `doctor`.
  **They all agree.** After v0.6.2 this is now true; two releases ago it was not.

- **The ambient hook correctly stayed silent.** At day 1 the memory sat at ~92% recall — nothing
  alarming to say, so it said nothing. The anti-nag threshold works.

- **The loop closed.** For the first time in a session I have driven end to end.

## WHAT CONFUSED ME

- **The dashboard has two adjacent sections both called "Retention."** *"Retention — recall by
  days since you first learned it"* is immediately followed by *"Retention by memory strength."*
  A user cannot tell which one is the real number. The second is the old v0.5 view and should be
  renamed or demoted.

## WHAT ANNOYED ME — and what I would have quit over

- **⚠ The curriculum architect took ~7 minutes, with zero feedback.** A blank terminal, no
  spinner, no "this takes a minute", nothing. **This is the single most likely place a first-time
  user closes the tab** — and it happens *before they have seen a single thing the product does
  well.* I knew what was happening and I still checked twice whether it had hung. A stranger will
  not wait.

  This is the highest-value UX fix in the product and it is one line of prose in `/learn`.

- **⚠ `decay` with nothing due tells you reviewing is pointless.** Right after encoding, with
  nothing yet due, it says:

  > *"1 concept encoded; 0.4 expected to survive 30 days untouched, 0.4 if reviewed today
  > (0 minutes) — a difference of 0.0"*

  That is **arithmetically correct** (nothing is due, so there is nothing to review today) and
  **rhetorically the exact opposite of the truth.** A user reads *"a difference of 0.0"* and
  concludes reviewing buys nothing. This is the same bug class the release is named for — a
  number that misleads — just pointing the other way.

## WOULD A STRANGER GET THROUGH THIS?

**Yes — if they survive the first seven minutes.** Everything after the wait is excellent and
several things are genuinely unmatched. But the product's worst moment is its *first* moment, and
it is a silent one.

## VERDICT

**Ship** — v0.6.2 is correct, and every number it reports is true.

**But the two ⚠ items above are the next release**, and the seven-minute silence is not a polish
item. It is the difference between a user who sees what this thing can do and one who never does.
